# Scout — Voice & Tone Guide
**Version 1.0 · April 2026**

---

## Who Scout Is

Scout is your photography mentor — the kind who's shot a thousand rolls of film, has strong opinions about light, and somehow makes you feel like *you* have great instincts too. Scout is warm without being gushing, encouraging without being a cheerleader, and just campy enough to be charming. Scout never lectures. Scout nudges.

Think: a creative friend who happens to be really good at this. They'll push you to look at something differently, celebrate the fact that you showed up at all, and never make you feel bad for missing a day.

**Scout is not:**
- A coach running drills
- A wellness app telling you to "practice self-care"
- A tutorial channel
- An AI assistant explaining its reasoning

---

## Core Voice Attributes

### 1. Mentoring, not instructing
Scout guides with a light hand. The goal is to spark an idea, not deliver a lesson.

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|-------|----------|
| "Find the oldest thing near you." | "To practice observational skills, look for aged objects in your environment." |
| "Today: one shadow, nothing else." | "Shadows can create compelling compositions. Try isolating one." |
| "Something's hiding in plain sight. Find it." | "Look for overlooked details in your immediate surroundings." |

---

### 2. Warm but not gushing
Encouragement should feel real, not performed. Scout means it.

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|-------|----------|
| "Day 7. You're building something." | "Amazing work! You've completed an entire week! 🎉" |
| "Good eye." | "Fantastic shot! You're so talented!" |
| "You showed up. That's the whole game." | "Every photo you take is a beautiful gift to your future self!" |

---

### 3. Charming, a little campy
Scout has personality. A dry observation, a slightly dramatic framing, a touch of theater — it's welcome.

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|-------|----------|
| "Dramatic lighting. Go find some." | "Look for interesting lighting conditions." |
| "The mundane is hiding something. Investigate." | "Everyday objects can make great subjects." |
| "Seven days. Not bad at all." | "You completed your first week!" |

---

### 4. Encouraging and positive
Scout always believes you'll get the shot. Even on empty states and missed days, the tone is open — never guilt-inducing.

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|-------|----------|
| "Start here. Start now." | "You haven't taken a photo yet!" |
| "Every streak starts with one." | "Don't break your streak!" |
| "No photo today — yet." | "You missed today's photo." |

---

### 5. Short, declarative, concrete
Scout doesn't over-explain. If a sentence has a subordinate clause, cut it. If it explains its own reasoning, cut it. The best Scout copy lands in one beat.

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|-------|----------|
| "Shoot from the floor up." | "Try shooting from a lower angle, which can make ordinary subjects feel more dramatic." |
| "One frame. No people." | "For today's prompt, consider taking a photo without any people in the frame." |
| "What does quiet look like?" | "Think about how you might represent the concept of quietness visually." |

## Tone by Context

### Prompt screen (shoot moment)
The user is about to pick up their camera. This is the highest-stakes copy in the app.

- **Tone:** Confident, playful, direct. A little spark.
- **Length:** One sentence. Under 120 characters.
- **Register:** Declarative or imperative. Present tense.
- **Goal:** Eliminate decision paralysis. Give them one thing to look for.

> *"Find a long shadow and walk toward it."*
> *"Something ordinary is doing something strange today. Find it."*
> *"Shoot what you'd normally walk past."*

---

### Milestone / week complete
The user finished something. This is a moment of quiet celebration — not a confetti explosion.

- **Tone:** Warm, a little theatrical, genuine.
- **Length:** Short headline + optional one-liner.
- **Goal:** Make them feel good without being hollow about it.

> *"Seven days."* (the number speaks for itself)
> *"You built something this week."*
> *"A full week. That's the practice."*

---

### Empty state / Day One
The user hasn't taken a photo yet — today or ever. No pressure, just an invitation.

- **Tone:** Open, easy, slightly wry.
- **Goal:** Make starting feel small and doable.

> *"Day one starts whenever you shoot."*
> *"No photo yet. There's still time."*
> *"Start here."*

---

### Settings / account (functional UI)
Lower stakes, practical. Scout's personality is present but quiet.

- **Tone:** Friendly and plain.
- **Goal:** Be clear. Don't be boring, but don't perform either.

> *"Change Password"* (not "Update your password here")
> *"Sign Out"* (not "Log out of your account")
> *"Download All Photos"* (not "Export your photo archive")

---

### Error states
Something went wrong. Scout stays calm and doesn't blame.

- **Tone:** Matter-of-fact, brief, with a light touch.
- **Goal:** Tell them what happened and what to do. Don't catastrophize.

> *"Couldn't load your photo. Try again."*
> *"Something went sideways. Give it a moment."*
> *"No connection right now."*

## Word List

### Scout says
- *find* / *look for* / *notice*
- *today* / *right now* / *near you*
- *shoot* / *frame* / *photograph*
- *something* / *one thing* / *a single*
- *yet* (for empty states — keeps them open)
- *practice* / *building* / *showing up*

### Scout never says
- *utilize* / *leverage* / *capture*
- *amazing* / *incredible* / *fantastic*
- *journey* / *adventure* / *explore your creativity*
- *beautiful* (about the user's photos — too hollow)
- *content* (as a noun referring to photos)
- *curate* / *curation*
- *mindful* / *intentional* / *authentic*
- *don't forget* / *remember to*

## Example Rewrites

### Prompt copy

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| "Photograph the way evening light grazes across tree bark or weathered surfaces near you right now, capturing the texture and shadows at a close angle while the partly cloudy sky still holds some color on the horizon." | *"Find a surface the evening light is just barely touching."* |
| "Look for interesting patterns in your everyday environment that you might otherwise overlook." | *"Something near you has a pattern. Get close."* |
| "Today, consider the relationship between light and shadow in your immediate environment." | *"Chase the shadow, not the light."* |

### Milestone copy

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| "Congratulations! You've completed your first full week!" | *"Seven days. You're building something real."* |
| "Amazing work — you've taken photos 7 days in a row!" | *"A full week. That's the whole practice right there."* |

### Empty state copy

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| "You haven't taken a photo yet today!" | *"No photo yet. Still time."* |
| "Start your visual record by taking your first photo." | *"Day one starts here."* |

## Prompt Format Rules

These apply to all daily photography prompts — whether written by the content strategist, generated by AI, or both.

- **Length:** One sentence. Hard limit: 120 characters.
- **Tense:** Present or imperative ("Find…" / "Shoot…" / "Look for…")
- **Structure:** One subject, one situation. Not two connected ideas.
- **No subordinate clauses.** If it has a comma, read it again.
- **No technique explanation.** Trust the photographer.
- **Concrete noun required.** "A shadow," "your hands," "a doorway" — not "something interesting."
- **Must work anywhere.** City, suburb, indoors, outdoors, any weather.

**Self-check:** Read it out loud. If it sounds like a lesson, cut it. If it sounds like a dare, keep it.
