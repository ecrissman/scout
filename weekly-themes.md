# Sightful — Weekly Photography Themes

52 themes for the year. Each is a creative brief — a way of seeing to carry with you for seven days. Themes are aspirational, not instructional. The *how* lives in the daily tips.

---

| # | Week | Theme | Description |
|---|------|-------|-------------|
| 1 | W01 | **New Light** | The world looks different in January — find something ordinary made strange by winter light. |
| 2 | W02 | **Negative Space** | Let emptiness do the work. Look for what surrounds your subject, not the subject itself. |
| 3 | W03 | **Texture Up Close** | Move closer than feels comfortable and let surface become the entire story. |
| 4 | W04 | **Borrowed Light** | Seek only light that has traveled through or reflected off something else before it reached your subject. |
| 5 | W05 | **The Moment Before** | Stop reacting. Start anticipating. The photograph exists a half-second before most people press the shutter. |
| 6 | W06 | **Edges & Boundaries** | Look for where two things meet — shadows, materials, moods, seasons. The edge is where things get interesting. |
| 7 | W07 | **Repetition** | Find a rhythm in the world. Then find the thing that breaks it. The break is your subject. |
| 8 | W08 | **Still Life** | Arrange or discover three objects together and photograph them as if they belong to each other. |
| 9 | W09 | **From Below** | Get low and look up. Subjects that feel ordinary at eye level become something else entirely from the ground. |
| 10 | W10 | **From Above** | Now go the other direction. Look straight down at the world. Compare what you felt last week — same instinct, opposite result. |
| 11 | W11 | **Reading the Light** | Before you shoot, stop. Describe the light to yourself — where it comes from, how it falls, what it does to your subject. See first. Shoot second. |
| 12 | W12 | **One Color** | Commit to a single dominant hue and build the whole frame around it. |
| 13 | W13 | **Morning** | Only shoot in the first two hours after sunrise. Something about early light changes everything — find out what it is for you. |
| 14 | W14 | **Hands** | Photograph hands — working, resting, holding, gesturing. Hands tell the stories faces try to hide. |
| 15 | W15 | **Layers** | Build depth. Foreground, middle ground, background — all three present, all three in conversation. |
| 16 | W16 | **Found Typography** | Letters and numbers that exist in the world — signs, stamps, worn paint, pressed metal. Language as visual object. |
| 17 | W17 | **Rain or Shine** | Let the weather be the subject, not the obstacle. Whatever today brings — work with it, not around it. |
| 18 | W18 | **Doors & Thresholds** | Entrances, exits, gates — the architecture of beginning and end. What lies just beyond the frame? |
| 19 | W19 | **Movement** | Let things move. Stop trying to freeze the world in place and see what happens when you let time show itself. |
| 20 | W20 | **Golden Hour** | Chase that last hour of light before sunset. This week is about learning to see what warm, directional light does to the world. |
| 21 | W21 | **Solitude** | One person, alone in a space — not lonely, just present. The quiet between people is its own subject. |
| 22 | W22 | **Geometry** | Triangles, circles, grids — find the shapes hiding inside ordinary scenes. |
| 23 | W23 | **Green** | Spring at its peak — find every shade, every texture, every depth of green you can. |
| 24 | W24 | **Shadows** | The shadow is the subject. The thing casting it is secondary. |
| 25 | W25 | **What's Left Behind** | Objects, traces, marks — evidence of something or someone that was here and isn't anymore. |
| 26 | W26 | **Halfway Through** | Midyear. Photograph something that represents where you are right now — not where you started, not where you're going. |
| 27 | W27 | **Heat & Light** | The hard, flat light of summer that photographers usually avoid. Find beauty in the difficult. |
| 28 | W28 | **Small World** | Find the universe in something tiny. The smaller you go, the stranger and more complete the world becomes. |
| 29 | W29 | **Urban Nature** | Plants, animals, sky, and water asserting themselves inside the city. Nature doesn't wait for permission. |
| 30 | W30 | **Reflection** | Mirrors, windows, puddles, sunglasses — a world seen through duplication. Which version is more true? |
| 31 | W31 | **Open Window** | Shoot from inside looking out, or frame your subject within a window. The boundary between inside and outside is its own kind of composition. |
| 32 | W32 | **Late Light** | Long shadows, low sun, everything gilded and stretched. Summer's light at its most generous. |
| 33 | W33 | **Imperfection** | A crack, a stain, a repair, a worn edge — beauty in what isn't pristine. Perfection is rarely the most interesting thing in the room. |
| 34 | W34 | **Connection** | Two things relating to each other — people, objects, or elements in quiet conversation. |
| 35 | W35 | **The Everyday** | Your kitchen, your commute, your desk. Make the mundane worth looking at. |
| 36 | W36 | **Isolation** | Find one thing and let everything else fall away. Separation — not blur — is the point. |
| 37 | W37 | **Transition Season** | Summer giving way to autumn — photograph the overlap, the in-between, the not-yet. |
| 38 | W38 | **Symmetry** | Find a frame that holds together on both sides — then decide whether to honor it or break it. |
| 39 | W39 | **Waiting** | A moment of pause — someone or something suspended between two things. The held breath before what comes next. |
| 40 | W40 | **Fog & Atmosphere** | Mist, smoke, steam, haze — let the air itself become a presence in the frame. |
| 41 | W41 | **Autumn Color** | One week where the light and the leaves conspire. Don't miss it. |
| 42 | W42 | **Silhouette** | Strip your subject down to shape and outline alone. Form without detail. |
| 43 | W43 | **Portraits Without Faces** | Tell a story about a person without showing their face. What else do we carry that says who we are? |
| 44 | W44 | **Warm Interiors** | As the outside cools, turn inward — candlelight, lamplight, kitchen glow. The light we make for ourselves. |
| 45 | W45 | **Decay & Change** | Things aging, weathering, transforming — time made visible in surfaces and edges. |
| 46 | W46 | **Night** | After dark — find the light sources and let darkness shape what remains. |
| 47 | W47 | **Available Light Portraits** | No artificial light. Just a person and whatever light the room offers. Learn to see light before you try to control it. |
| 48 | W48 | **Street Level** | Sidewalks, gutters, feet, curbs — everything at ground height. Look at the world from where it lives. |
| 49 | W49 | **Ceremony** | Any ritual — coffee in the morning, a commute, a familiar route walked again. The sacred hiding in the ordinary. |
| 50 | W50 | **Long Exposure** | Let time accumulate inside a single frame. What does the world look like when a moment lasts longer than a moment? |
| 51 | W51 | **Year's End** | What does closing out look like — last light, last leaves, last warmth before the turn. |
| 52 | W52 | **Begin Again** | The cusp of a new year — photograph something that feels like a beginning. |
